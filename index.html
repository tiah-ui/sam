The Man Behind The Mic

Who is Sammy Forson?

Sammy Forson is one of Ghana's most decorated broadcast personalities. Born in Agona Swedru, raised partly in Zambia, his journey began not in a radio studio — but behind a computer. He was the man who introduced digital music systems to Ghana's radio stations, physically transforming how the country's airwaves operated.

Over 21 years, he moved through Garden City Radio, Citi 97.3FM, YFM, Live 91.9FM and Joy 99.7FM — building a loyal following at every stop. At his peak at YFM, his mid-morning show commanded audiences across Accra, Kumasi and Takoradi simultaneously.

"Bra Sammy Wossop" — what callers said the moment they got through. Every time. For 21 years.

Beyond radio, he served as Business Development Manager for Sarkodie — Ghana's biggest rapper — from 2010 to 2015, spearheading the campaigns behind Rapperholic, Sarkology and Mary, and securing deals with Samsung, Fan Milk and more. He helped build a BET Award winner.

Now based in the UK, Sammy stepped away from media in late 2023 after 21 years. The gap he left is still felt.

Spotify and Apple Music pay per stream. A well-curated playlist with 5,000+ followers generates passive income monthly. At 10,000 streams/month, this compounds as the audience grows.
£30–£150 / month at scale

Podcast Sponsorships
At 5,000 monthly downloads, African-diaspora-focused sponsors (telcos, remittance services, African fashion/food brands) will pay £20–£40 CPM. One sponsor per episode at 5,000 downloads = £100–£200/episode.
£400–£800 / month (5k downloads)

Artist Pitch Submissions
Artists and labels pay a curation consideration fee (£50–£150) for playlist placement review. This is standard practice for established curators. At 20 submissions/month, this alone covers the equipment cost monthly.
£1,000–£3,000 / month at scale
🤝
Brand Partnerships
Dedicated brand deals with companies targeting the African diaspora. MTN, Airtel, Afrobeats festival sponsors, African fashion brands, food delivery apps. One deal at this audience level = £500–£2,000.
£500–£5,000 / deal
📱
YouTube Ad Revenue
Once the YouTube channel hits 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours (achievable in 3–6 months with consistent content), ad revenue activates. African music content typically commands a strong CPM from global advertisers.
£200–£1,000 / month

Live Events
Quarterly diaspora listening parties and live podcast recordings in London. A 200-person event at £30 ticket = £6,000 gross. As the brand scales, these become the highest-margin revenue stream.
